Why it matters

Decision friction. Do you have it?

Every business reaches a point where the questions outnumber the answers. Too many opinions, too much risk, not enough clarity to move forward with confidence. The decision is there — what's missing is the evidence to make it.

Most product owners and designers already understand the value of user research. The barriers are often more practical — cost, time, competing priorities. But deprioritising research doesn't make the uncertainty go away. It just means decisions get made without the evidence they deserve.

The cost of not speaking to your users rarely shows up all at once. It's the feature nobody uses, the launch that underdelivers, the customer who dropped off at sign-up or checkout before you understood why. Rework, delays, reputational damage all carry a cost — one that evidence-led decisions can help avoid. It's no coincidence that the world's most successful businesses — at every scale — have customer understanding at their core.
